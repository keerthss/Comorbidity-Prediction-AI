Heart Disease: Understanding Risks & Early Prediction

March 23, 2025 by Health Insights

Heart disease is a broad term covering various cardiovascular conditions that affect the heart's function. It is a leading cause of death worldwide, but **early detection and lifestyle modifications** can significantly reduce risks and improve heart health.

Common Types of Heart Disease

  • Coronary Artery Disease (CAD): A condition caused by plaque buildup in the arteries, leading to reduced blood flow to the heart.
  • Heart Attack (Myocardial Infarction): A blockage in the coronary arteries that prevents oxygen from reaching the heart muscle.
  • Arrhythmia: Irregular heartbeats that can cause dizziness, palpitations, or even sudden cardiac arrest.
  • Heart Failure: A condition where the heart is unable to pump blood effectively, leading to fatigue, swelling, and breathing difficulties.
  • Valvular Heart Disease: Issues with heart valves that affect blood flow efficiency.

Key Risk Factors

Several factors contribute to the risk of developing heart disease, including:

  • High Blood Pressure (Hypertension): Puts excessive strain on the heart.
  • High Cholesterol: Leads to plaque buildup in arteries.
  • Diabetes: Increases the likelihood of cardiovascular complications.
  • Smoking: Damages blood vessels and reduces oxygen supply.
  • Obesity & Inactivity: Excess weight puts added stress on the heart.
  • Family History: Genetics play a role in heart disease susceptibility.

Heart Disease Diagnosis & Indicators

Indicator Normal Range Risk Level
Blood Pressure (mmHg) 120/80 Above 130/90 (Hypertension)
Cholesterol (mg/dL) Total < 200 Above 240 (High Risk)
Resting Heart Rate (bpm) 60 - 100 Above 100 (Tachycardia)
Body Mass Index (BMI) 18.5 - 24.9 Above 30 (Obese)
