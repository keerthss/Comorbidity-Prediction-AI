Kidney Disease: Understanding the Condition & Managing the Risks

March 23, 2025 by Health Insights

Kidney disease, also known as **chronic kidney disease (CKD),** occurs when the kidneys lose their ability to effectively filter waste from the blood. This can lead to **fluid buildup, electrolyte imbalances, and serious health complications.** Early detection and proper management can slow disease progression and prevent kidney failure.

Common Symptoms of Kidney Disease

  • Fatigue & Weakness: Due to toxin buildup and anemia.
  • Swelling (Edema): Fluid retention in the legs, ankles, and face.
  • Changes in Urination: Increased or decreased frequency, foamy urine, or blood in urine.
  • High Blood Pressure: Poor kidney function affects blood pressure regulation.
  • Loss of Appetite & Nausea: Accumulation of waste in the bloodstream.
  • Shortness of Breath: Fluid overload in the lungs.

Diagnosis & Key Indicators

Test Normal Indicates Kidney Disease
Glomerular Filtration Rate (GFR) > 90 mL/min < 60 mL/min (Chronic Kidney Disease)
Serum Creatinine (mg/dL) 0.6 - 1.2 Above 1.2 (Impaired Kidney Function)
Blood Urea Nitrogen (BUN) (mg/dL) 7 - 20 Above 20 (Possible Kidney Dysfunction)
Urine Albumin-to-Creatinine Ratio (UACR) < 30 mg/g > 30 mg/g (Proteinuria - Kidney Damage)
