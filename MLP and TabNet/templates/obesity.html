Obesity: Understanding the Condition & Managing the Risks

March 23, 2025 by Health Insights

Obesity is a chronic health condition characterized by excessive body fat accumulation that increases the risk of **heart disease, diabetes, high blood pressure, and other health issues.** It is often caused by a combination of genetic, behavioral, and environmental factors.

Common Health Risks of Obesity

  • Heart Disease & Stroke: Excess weight increases blood pressure and cholesterol levels.
  • Type 2 Diabetes: Obesity is a major risk factor for insulin resistance.
  • Joint Pain & Osteoarthritis: Extra weight puts stress on the joints, leading to wear and tear.
  • Sleep Apnea & Breathing Issues: Fat deposits around the neck can obstruct breathing during sleep.
  • Digestive Issues: Increased risk of GERD (acid reflux) and fatty liver disease.
  • Depression & Low Self-Esteem: Mental health effects due to societal stigma and body image concerns.

Diagnosis & Key Indicators

Measure Normal Indicates Obesity
Body Mass Index (BMI) 18.5 - 24.9 > 30 (Obese)
Waist Circumference < 40 inches (Men) < 35 inches (Women) > 40 inches (Men) > 35 inches (Women)
Body Fat Percentage 14 - 24% (Men) / 21 - 31% (Women) > 25% (Men) / > 32% (Women)
