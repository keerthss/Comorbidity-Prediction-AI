Parkinson’s Disease: Symptoms, Causes & Early Detection

March 23, 2025 by Health Insights

Parkinson’s disease is a progressive neurological disorder that affects movement. It occurs when **dopamine-producing neurons** in the brain degenerate over time. Although there is no cure, **early detection and proper management** can significantly improve quality of life.

Common Symptoms of Parkinson’s Disease

  • Tremors: Uncontrollable shaking, usually in the hands or fingers.
  • Bradykinesia (Slowed Movement): Difficulty initiating movements or experiencing a slowing down over time.
  • Muscle Rigidity: Stiffness in the limbs or torso.
  • Postural Instability: Difficulty maintaining balance and coordination.
  • Speech Changes: Soft, slurred, or hesitant speech patterns.

Key Risk Factors

While the exact cause of Parkinson’s remains unknown, several factors contribute to the risk:

  • Age: Most cases occur after 60, though early-onset Parkinson’s can appear before 50.
  • Genetics: Family history can increase susceptibility.
  • Environmental Triggers: Exposure to toxins like pesticides or heavy metals.
  • Brain Injuries: Repeated head trauma may increase risk.

Parkinson’s Diagnosis & Key Indicators

Indicator Normal Condition Possible Parkinson’s Risk
Hand Tremors No noticeable tremors Shaking, even at rest
Muscle Stiffness Normal flexibility Increased resistance in movement
Walking Pattern Normal stride and posture Shuffling gait, stooped posture
Speech Clear and strong voice Soft, slurred, or monotone voice
