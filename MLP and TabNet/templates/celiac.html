Celiac Disease: Understanding the Condition & Managing the Risks

March 23, 2025 by Health Insights

Celiac disease is an autoimmune disorder where the ingestion of gluten triggers an immune response that damages the small intestine. This damage can lead to **nutrient malabsorption**, digestive issues, and long-term health complications. However, with **early detection and a strict gluten-free diet,** individuals with celiac disease can lead healthy lives.

Symptoms of Celiac Disease

  • Digestive Issues: Bloating, diarrhea, constipation, and abdominal pain.
  • Fatigue: Chronic tiredness due to poor nutrient absorption.
  • Weight Loss: Involuntary weight loss due to malabsorption.
  • Skin Issues: Dermatitis herpetiformis (itchy rash linked to gluten ingestion).
  • Bone & Joint Pain: Increased risk of osteoporosis due to calcium deficiency.
  • Neurological Symptoms: Headaches, depression, anxiety, and brain fog.

Diagnosis & Indicators

Test Normal Indicates Celiac Disease
Tissue Transglutaminase Antibody (tTG-IgA) Negative Positive
Endomysial Antibody (EMA-IgA) Negative Positive
Deamidated Gliadin Peptide (DGP-IgA/IgG) Negative Positive
Small Intestine Biopsy Normal Villi Flattened Villi (Damage Present)
