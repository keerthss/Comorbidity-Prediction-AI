Hypertension: Causes, Risks & Early Detection

March 23, 2025 by Health Insights

Hypertension, commonly known as high blood pressure, is a chronic condition where the **force of blood against the artery walls** remains consistently too high. Left untreated, it can lead to **heart disease, stroke, kidney damage, and other complications**. Early detection and lifestyle modifications play a crucial role in managing hypertension effectively.

Common Symptoms of Hypertension

Hypertension is often called the **“silent killer”** because it may not cause noticeable symptoms until it becomes severe. Some warning signs include:

  • Headaches: Persistent or severe headaches, especially in the morning.
  • Dizziness: Lightheadedness or balance issues.
  • Blurred Vision: Difficulty seeing clearly.
  • Shortness of Breath: Feeling breathless even with mild activity.
  • Chest Pain: Occurs in extreme cases when the heart is affected.
  • Nosebleeds: Rare but may occur in very high blood pressure cases.

Key Risk Factors

Several factors increase the likelihood of developing hypertension, including:

  • High Sodium Intake: Excess salt can cause fluid retention, raising blood pressure.
  • Obesity: Excess weight puts extra strain on the heart and arteries.
  • Lack of Physical Activity: A sedentary lifestyle contributes to high blood pressure.
  • Smoking & Alcohol: Both can constrict blood vessels and elevate blood pressure.
  • Chronic Stress: Leads to prolonged high blood pressure levels.
  • Genetic Factors: Family history can increase the risk of hypertension.

Hypertension Diagnosis & Key Indicators
